If you're weighing whether to become a barber, you deserve to know who's guiding the decision. American Barber Institute has helped New Yorkers trade uncertain jobs for a licensed, portable trade since 1996 — this is who we are and why our guidance is worth trusting.

The short answer

American Barber Institute is a New York State-licensed barbering school founded in 1996 and based in Midtown Manhattan, with a second campus in the Bronx. Every instructor is a former ABI student, the program is the full 500-hour Master Barber curriculum required for a New York license, and graduates go on to work at — and own — barbershops across the city. We built this guide site so career-changers can research the trade honestly before committing.

What We Believe

A Trade You Own for Life

Our mission is to prepare people to become thoroughly qualified barbers, ready for lifetime careers in barbering — not a job you clock into, but a licensed skill you carry anywhere.

We believe a barber's license is one of the most portable, resilient credentials in the workforce: it can't be offshored, it doesn't expire when a company downsizes, and it lets you work for a shop, rent a chair, or open your own place. That's why we frame barbering as a genuine career change rather than a course.

When graduates want it, we help them find work in the field and develop the mindset to keep it. We've earned a reputation with shop owners as a reliable source of quality professionals — which is exactly why our graduates get hired. How the Path Works

From Deciding to Licensed, in Four Steps

The route into barbering in New York is more predictable than most career changes. Here's the whole arc.

  1. 1

    Decide it fits your life

    Figure out the money, the hours and the timeline before you commit. Our FAQ and funding guide exist for exactly this stage.

  2. 2

    Complete the 500-hour program

    New York requires 500 hours for a Master Barber license. Full-time, that's roughly 4 months; on weekends, about 6–7 months. You choose the pace.

  3. 3

    Pass the NY State Board Exam

    The curriculum is built around the written and practical exam. Passing it makes you a licensed Master Barber, legally able to work anywhere in New York.

  4. 4

    Start earning behind the chair

    Work for a shop, rent a chair, or open your own. See the real shops our graduates joined and built.

Is It Right For You

What Makes a Good Fit

Barbering isn't for everyone — and an honest guide says so. It tends to suit people who:

  • Like working with people and don't want a desk job
  • Want a licensed skill they can take to any city or state
  • Value flexible hours and the option to be their own boss
  • Prefer a hands-on trade over more years of general schooling
  • Want to start earning in months, not years

What You'd Need To Begin

The Entry Bar Is Low

One reason barbering is such an accessible career change: the requirements to start training in New York are modest.

  • A Social Security card and valid photo ID
  • A High School Diploma or GED — or pass the ATB entrance exam
  • To be at least 17 years of age
  • No prior experience — beginners start from zero every month
