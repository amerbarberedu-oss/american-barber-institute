How It Works

Transferring Your NY Barber License to Connecticut

Connecticut typically requires around 1,000 training hours, but New York hours can be credited toward reciprocity. Here's the path most students follow.

1

Meet Eligibility Requirements

Complete a state-approved barber program and hold a valid, current New York barber license in good standing.

2

Apply for Reciprocity

Submit an application to the Connecticut Department of Public Health with proof of your New York license, documentation of your training hours and the applicable processing fees.

3

Pass Any Required Exam

You may need to pass Connecticut's state-specific barber exam covering local health, safety and professional standards — many students find their New York training fully prepares them for it.

4

Receive Your CT License

Once approved, you're granted a Connecticut barber license and can legally work in the state — the process is designed to minimize downtime between graduation and your first chair.
