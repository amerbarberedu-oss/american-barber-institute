The Short Answer

Who is the 50-hour refresher for?

The 50-hour refresher is the fast track for people who already have hair experience on paper. If you're a licensed cosmetologist or hairdresser, or a barber apprentice, you can complete 50 hours of focused training — roughly two weeks — and sit for the New York State Board exam to earn your Master Barber license. It is not for complete beginners: if you have no prior license, you need the full 500-hour route instead.

One thing to know: the refresher is offered at the Manhattan training location (48 West 39th Street, New York, NY 10018), not at every campus.

Common Questions

The Refresher Route, Answered

Do I still have to take the state exam after the refresher?
Yes. The refresher gets you exam-ready; you then sit for the New York State Board barber exam. Passing it earns your Master Barber license.
I'm a complete beginner — can I take the 50-hour course?
No. The refresher assumes existing licensure or apprentice experience. Beginners need the 500-hour program, which is built from the ground up.
Is the refresher available at every location?
It is offered at the Manhattan training location only (48 West 39th Street). The full 500-hour route is available more widely.
