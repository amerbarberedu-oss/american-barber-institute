NY Barber License Requirements (2026): Hours, Eligibility, Exam & Fees

Exactly what New York State requires to license a barber — the hours, the application, the exam, the costs, and how to transfer a license from another state.

By David Ayeoribe, Lead Senior Instructor & Director, American Barber Institute · Last updated 2026

Short answer

To get a New York barber license you must be at least 17, complete 500 hours of approved barber training, and pass the New York State barbering licensing examination (written + practical). You then apply for the Master Barber license through the New York Department of State, Division of Licensing Services, and pay the required licensing fee. There is no college requirement. Licensed barbers from other states may qualify to transfer, but New York does not offer blanket automatic reciprocity — the state evaluates your training and experience case by case.

The big one

The 500-hour training requirement

The single largest requirement is 500 hours of approved barber training. This is fixed by the state — it is not something a school can shorten. Those hours combine hands-on practice on real clients with the classroom theory the written exam covers.

Definition — Master Barber license: New York's full barbering license. It authorizes you to perform all barbering services for pay and to own a barbershop and employ barbers and apprentices. It is the credential most students are working toward.

The test

The licensing exam

Completing 500 hours makes you eligible to test; passing the exam is what earns the license. The New York State barbering licensing examination has two components:

Written test

Sanitation and sterilization, disease control, skin and hair structure, safe practices, and New York regulations.

Practical test

A supervised demonstration of core barbering skills and proper sanitation.

Programs that build test prep into the curriculum give you the best shot at passing on the first attempt, because the exam simply confirms the work you've been doing on the floor. See our companion guide on what a licensed barber earns once you're through it.

Budgeting

Fees & costs

There are two cost buckets to plan for, and they're separate:

Training cost

What you pay a school for the 500-hour program. Varies by school and is far below college tuition; many programs offer weekly payment plans. See funding options.

State licensing fee

A modest fee paid to the New York Department of State when you apply for the license itself, plus any exam-related fees. Always check the current amount on the state's official site, as fees change.

Because exact state fees change periodically, we deliberately don't quote a dollar figure here — verify it directly with the New York Department of State so you're working from the current number.

Moving to NY

Transferring a barber license to New York (reciprocity)

If you're already a licensed barber in another state, you may be able to obtain a New York license without repeating the entire process — but New York does not grant automatic, blanket reciprocity. Instead, the state reviews your existing license, training hours and work experience to decide whether they satisfy New York's requirements. In practice:

  • Barbers with substantial documented training and experience often qualify to license in New York after applying and, in some cases, passing the state exam.
  • If your original training fell short of New York's 500-hour standard, you may need additional hours to close the gap.
  • Applicants trained abroad should expect to submit translated, verified documentation.

Because these determinations are made case by case, the reliable move is to contact the New York Department of State, Division of Licensing Services, with your credentials before you assume you're covered.

One statewide license

Do requirements differ by borough?

No. The New York State Master Barber license is statewide — the same 500 hours and the same exam apply whether you'll work in the Bronx, Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens or anywhere else in New York. There is no separate borough or city license to chase.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How many hours do you need for a barber license in New York?

500 hours of approved barber training, documented, before you can sit the state licensing exam.

What's the minimum age to get a NY barber license?

You generally must be at least 17 years old to enroll in training and pursue the license.

Do I need a high-school diploma?

It helps, but most licensed schools admit students who pass an entrance exam instead of holding a diploma or GED.

Does New York have barber license reciprocity with other states?

Not automatically. New York reviews an out-of-state barber's license, hours and experience individually and may require additional hours or the state exam. Contact the NY Department of State to confirm your specific case.

How much does the NY barber license cost?

There's a modest state licensing fee (plus exam fees) paid to the New York Department of State, separate from what you pay a school for training. Verify the current fee on the state's official site.

Is the license valid across all of New York?

Yes. The Master Barber license is statewide — one license covers all five NYC boroughs and the rest of the state.
