The NY Contagious Diseases Course: What It Is & Who Needs It

New York requires a short Transmission of Contagious Diseases course for every new barber operator and apprentice application — and every renewal. Here's exactly what it covers, who has to take it, and how to complete it entirely from home.

The Short Answer

Do you need the Contagious Diseases course?

Yes — if you are applying for, or renewing, a New York Barber Operator or Barber Apprentice license, this course is required. New York mandates a program of study on the Transmission of Contagious Diseases for anyone who wants to be employed in a barbershop. It is a 3-hour home-study course you complete by mail, and it ends with two certificates of completion — one for your license application and one for your records.

This requirement is separate from your main training hours. Whether you took the 500-hour route or the refresher route, this course is a distinct licensing box you still need to check.

Step by Step

How to Complete It by Mail

Four steps, all handled through the mail — no cohort, no start date, no travel.

Enroll

You receive the first packet — an enrollment agreement and the program booklet. Return the signed agreement with your payment.

Study

Once your signed agreement and payment arrive, the second packet with the exam is mailed to you. Read and study the booklet before testing.

Take the Exam

Complete the exam in full and mail it back — no in-person visit required.

Get Certified

Your exam is graded, and on passing you're mailed two certificates: one for your application, one to keep.

The Details

Why New York Requires It

Barbers work in close contact with clients, using tools that touch skin and hair. The state requires this coursework so every licensed operator understands how contagious conditions spread and how proper sanitation and sterilization prevent them. It is a public-health baseline — and it applies at initial licensure and again at renewal.

Because it is home study, it is the most flexible part of the whole licensing process. There is no cohort, no start date to wait for, and no travel — just the packet, the booklet and the mail.

Common Questions

Quick Answers

Does this replace my barber training hours?
No. It is an additional licensing requirement on top of your 500-hour or refresher training — not a substitute for it.
Do I retake it when I renew?
Yes. New York requires the contagious diseases coursework at renewal as well as at initial application.
Can I really do the whole thing by mail?
Yes — enrollment, study booklet, exam and certificates all happen through the mail. No in-person session is required.
