How to Become a Barber in New York: The Steps, Hours & License

The exact path from "I'm thinking about it" to licensed and working.

Short answer: To become a licensed barber in New York you complete a state-approved 500-hour Master Barber course, complete the required 3-hour HIV/AIDS & Contagious Diseases component, and pass the New York State Board exam. Full-time that takes about 4 months; on a weekend schedule about 6–7 months. Once you pass and are licensed, you can start working — and, with a Master Barber license, eventually own your own shop.

Thinking about a career change into barbering? The path is clearer than most people expect. Here's every step, in order, so you know exactly what becoming a barber in New York involves before you commit.

The steps to becoming a barber in New York

  1. Meet the basics. You need to be at least 17 and have the equivalent of an 8th-grade education. No prior experience required.
  2. Enroll in a 500-hour Master Barber program. New York requires 500 hours of state-approved training. This is where you learn cutting, fades, shaves, sanitation and the theory the exam tests.
  3. Complete the 3-hour Contagious Diseases course. New York also mandates a short HIV/AIDS & Contagious Diseases program to ensure you can maintain a safe, hygienic environment for clients.
  4. Pass the New York State Board exam. A written and practical test covering technique and safety.
  5. Get licensed and start working. Once licensed you can work behind a chair — and a Master Barber license also qualifies you to own a shop and employ barbers.

Because programs offer morning, afternoon and weekend options, most people fit training around their existing life — one of the reasons barbering is such an accessible career change.

What you'll actually learn

A good 500-hour program is far more than watching demos. You train on a real, diverse clientele and build a full toolkit: classic and modern cutting, fades and tapers, shear and razor work, lineups, shaves, color basics, sanitation, and the theory the State Board tests. Just as important, the strongest programs teach the business side — clientele-building and professionalism — so you graduate ready to earn, not just to pass. What does it cost — and can you afford it?

Barbering is one of the most accessible skilled trades: a licensed 500-hour NY program typically runs from around $4,500 to over $10,000, and most students pay with a weekly plan rather than a lump sum — often out of income they're still earning while on a flexible schedule. Veterans and eligible New Yorkers can also use funding programs. See the full breakdown in how much barber school costs in New York.

Is barbering a good career?

For the right person, yes: you can start earning right after licensing, set your own schedule, and grow toward booth rent or shop ownership with a high income ceiling. There's no guaranteed salary — but there's also no cap. See the real numbers in how much barbers make in New York.
