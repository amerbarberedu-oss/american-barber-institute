The 500-Hour Route to a New York Master Barber License

New York requires 500 hours of approved training before you can sit for the State Board exam. This is what those 500 hours actually look like — how long they take, what they cost, and how to decide if the full program is the right path for you.

The Short Answer

How long does it take, and what does it cost?

In New York, becoming a licensed Master Barber takes 500 hours of state-approved training. On a full-time schedule that is roughly 4 months; on a part-time weekend schedule it is closer to 6–7 months while you keep a weekday job. Tuition for a 500-hour program typically runs $4,600 to $5,600 depending on the schedule you choose. After you complete the hours, you sit for the New York State Board exam — and that is the credential employers and shop owners actually look for.

The 500-hour route is the standard path for career-changers with no prior license. If you are already a licensed cosmetologist or hold a barber license from another state, a shorter refresher path may apply instead — see the 50-hour refresher route.

The Timeline

What the 500 Hours Look Like

Four phases take you from your first day in the classroom to a licensed barber working the chair.

Theory First

Your first two weeks cover the fundamentals New York tests you on: sanitation, sterilization, barber law, shop management and barbering history.

On the Floor

The bulk of the hours are hands-on with real clients — fades, tapers, razor work, beard trims, hot-towel shaves and facial massage under instructor supervision.

Exam Prep

The final stretch is dedicated to New York State Board exam preparation so you walk into the practical and written test ready.

Get Licensed

You pass the State Board exam, receive your license, and step into a shop as a working Master Barber.

Before You Start

Who Can Enroll in a 500-Hour Program

New York's entry bar is low by design — barbering is one of the most accessible skilled trades to break into. To begin a 500-hour program you generally need:

Common Questions

The 500-Hour Route, Answered

Is 500 hours the same everywhere in New York?
Yes. New York State sets 500 hours as the training requirement to qualify for the barber licensing exam. What varies between programs is the schedule, the amount of real-client practice, and the quality of exam prep — not the hour count.
Can I work while completing the 500 hours?
Yes — that is exactly what the weekend schedule is for. Weekend students finish in roughly 6–7 months while keeping a weekday job. Full-time morning or afternoon schedules finish faster, in about 4 months.
Do I need experience before I start?
No prior experience is required for the 500-hour route. It is built for complete beginners and career-changers. If you already hold a cosmetology or out-of-state barber license, look at the shorter refresher path instead.
What happens after I finish the hours?
You sit for the New York State Board barber exam (practical and written). Once you pass, you receive your Master Barber license and can work in any New York shop — or eventually open your own.
