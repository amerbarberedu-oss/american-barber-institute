Before You Commit

The questions worth asking a person

Most people who reach out are still figuring out whether the trade fits their life. These are the ones worth getting answered by admissions — not a brochure.

"Can I actually afford it?"

We'll walk you through weekly payment plans, a manageable down payment, and whether you qualify for GI Bill® or ACCES-VR funding. See the full breakdown on tuition & funding.

"Will it fit my schedule?"

Morning, afternoon, and weekend tracks all lead to the same 500-hour license. Compare them on the schedules page and pick the pace that fits your job and family.

"How long until I'm working?"

Roughly 4 months full-time, or about 6–7 months on weekends, then the New York State exam. Details are in the 500-hour program overview.

"Is it too late for me?"

People change into barbering at every age. Beginners with zero experience start every month — classes begin the first Monday of every month.

Getting Here

Right in the middle of Midtown

The Manhattan campus sits a few short blocks from nearly every subway line in the city — easy to reach whether you're coming from the boroughs, New Jersey, or upstate.

By subway

The B, D, F, M, N, Q, R, W, S, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 and 7 trains all run within a few short blocks (Bryant Park, Times Square, and Herald Square stations).

Common Questions

Contacting ABI: quick answers

Can I just call instead of filling out the form?

Absolutely. Call the Manhattan campus at (212) 290-2289 (English) or (212) 290-0278 (Español), or the Bronx campus at (718) 676-0640. You can also email admission@abi.edu any time.

Can I visit the campus before I enroll?

Yes — walk-ins are welcome Monday through Friday, 8 AM to 8 PM at both the Manhattan (48 West 39th Street) and Bronx (121 Westchester Square) campuses. Come see the training floor and meet instructors before you decide.

Do you have Spanish-speaking admissions staff?

Sí. Call (212) 290-0278 for Spanish-language admissions, and both campuses offer bilingual instruction and support throughout the 500-hour program.

When can I start after I reach out?

New classes begin the first Monday of every month, so you're rarely more than a few weeks from a start date once you decide. See the schedules page for upcoming start dates and how each track is paced.

Can you help me figure out funding?

Yes. Admissions can walk you through weekly payment plans and check whether you qualify for GI Bill® benefits or ACCES-VR vocational funding. Start with the funding overview, then call with your situation.
