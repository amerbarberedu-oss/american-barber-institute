What to Ask Before You Commit

Most people who reach out are still figuring out whether the trade fits their life. These are the questions worth getting answered by a person, not a brochure:

  • "Can I afford it?"We'll walk you through weekly payment plans, GI Bill® and ACCES-VR funding for those who qualify.
  • "Will it fit my schedule?"Morning, afternoon and weekend tracks all lead to the same 500-hour license.
  • "How long until I'm working?"Roughly 4 months full-time, or about 6–7 months on weekends, then the state exam.
  • "Is it too late for me?"People change into barbering at every age. Beginners with zero experience start every month.

Getting There

Subway: The B, D, F, M, N, Q, R, W, S, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 and 7 trains all run within a few short blocks of the Manhattan campus (Bryant Park / Times Square / Herald Square stations).

Bus & rail: The M5, M7, M20, M42 and M104 stop within a block or two. The Port Authority Bus Terminal and Metro-North at Grand Central are minutes away.

The Timeline Question

How Fast Could You Be Licensed?

Barbering is one of the quickest legitimate career changes there is. Every track covers the same 500 state-required hours — you're only choosing how you spread them out:

  • Full-time (Morning or Afternoon)~4 months to complete 500 hours, then the state exam
  • Weekend~6–7 months — keep your day job while you train
  • New start dates monthlyClasses begin the first Monday of every month
