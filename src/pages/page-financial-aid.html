Your Options

Three Ways to Fund Barber School

ABI has trained New York barbers for decades, and we believe anyone with a passion for the craft should be able to afford quality training. Here's how qualified students cover the cost.

1

ACCES-VR

New York State's vocational rehabilitation program supports individuals with documented disabilities in obtaining and maintaining employment. If you qualify, ACCES-VR can cover your tuition, tools and supplies for barber training.

  • Tuition, tools & textbooks potentially covered
  • For New Yorkers with documented disabilities
About the ACCES-VR program →
2

Veterans GI Bill®

ABI accepts VA education benefits, including the Post-9/11 GI Bill® (Ch. 33), VR&E (Ch. 31), the Montgomery GI Bill® and DEA (Ch. 35). Benefits can cover all or part of your tuition, and we certify your enrollment with the VA.

  • Four VA benefit chapters accepted
  • Qualifying dependents may be eligible too
Veterans & VA benefits →
3

Weekly Payment Plans

Not eligible for a funded program? Every ABI schedule includes a built-in payment plan — pay a down payment at registration, then weekly payments from $150 while you attend the 500-Hour Master Barber Program.

  • Payment plans from $150 per week
  • Finish training in about 4 months

Common Questions

Financial Aid FAQs

Who qualifies for ACCES-VR funding?

ACCES-VR serves New Yorkers with documented disabilities that affect their ability to work. If you qualify, the program can cover tuition, tools and supplies. See our ACCES-VR page or call admissions to get started.

Which VA benefits does ABI accept?

ABI accepts the Post-9/11 GI Bill® (Ch. 33), Veteran Readiness & Employment (Ch. 31), the Montgomery GI Bill® and Dependents' Educational Assistance (Ch. 35). Learn more on our veterans page.

What if I don't qualify for ACCES-VR or VA benefits?

Every schedule includes a weekly payment plan starting at $150 per week, so you can pay as you train instead of paying everything up front.
