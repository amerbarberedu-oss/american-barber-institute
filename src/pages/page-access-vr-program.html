The Program

What ACCES-VR Can Do for You

For many aspiring barbers, the path to a state license is blocked by cost. ACCES-VR removes that barrier — supporting individuals with disabilities in obtaining and maintaining employment, and covering the cost of education for eligible clients. ABI students funded through ACCES-VR have gone on to become accomplished barbers, shop owners and instructors.

Education & Training

  • Training including tuition, related fees and required textbooks
  • Occupational tools and equipment
  • Tutor, reader and note-taker services
  • Work readiness training

Counseling & Support

  • Vocational counseling, guidance and assessments
  • Rehabilitation technology
  • Special transportation & adaptive driver training
  • Physical and mental restoration services

Career & Beyond

  • Job development, placement and coaching
  • Work try-out & on-the-job training
  • Goods, equipment and supplies for self-employment
  • Occupational and business licenses

Check Your Eligibility

Applicants must have a disability that affects their ability to work, be eligible for employment in the United States after their education, reside in New York State, be at least 14 years old and be able to participate in vocational rehabilitation services.

Common Questions

ACCES-VR FAQs

Does ACCES-VR really pay for barber school?

Yes — for eligible clients, ACCES-VR covers the cost of education, including tuition, related fees, required textbooks, and occupational tools and equipment, removing the financial barriers to training.

What languages can I apply in?

Applications for ACCES-VR services are available in English, Chinese, Haitian-Creole, Italian, Russian and Spanish.

Does support end when school ends?

No. ACCES-VR services extend to job development and placement, work try-outs, on-the-job training, job coaching — and even equipment, supplies and licenses for self-employment if you plan to open your own shop.

Are there other ways to fund my training?

Yes — see our financial aid overview. Veterans may qualify for GI Bill® benefits, and every student can use ABI's weekly payment plans from $150 per week.
