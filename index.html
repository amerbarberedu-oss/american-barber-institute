Why Barbering

Why people leave their old job for the chair

Barbering is one of the few careers where you can go from zero to licensed and working in months — not years — with real earning power and the freedom to be your own boss. Here's why it draws so many career-changers.

Fast to License

500 hours — about 4 months full-time. A real credential, quickly.

Real Income

Tips, clientele and booth or shop ownership add up over time.

Always in Demand

People will always need a haircut — a recession-resistant trade.

Be Your Own Boss

Rent a booth or open a shop and control your schedule and earnings.

Creative & Hands-On

Work with your hands and see the result the moment you're done.

No Experience Needed

We train complete beginners and career-changers from day one.

Career Earnings

What a Barber Career Can Pay

Barbering rewards the hours you put in and the clientele you build. Here's a realistic arc as your reputation grows.

YEAR 1 · Entry-Level
$35,000–$45,000

Starting out in a shop, building your clientele and refining your technique.

YEARS 2–3 · Established
$50,000–$70,000

Loyal clientele, faster service and higher earnings as your reputation grows.

YEAR 3+ · Booth Renter / Shop Owner
$75,000–$100,000+

Full control of your schedule and earnings — the path to true entrepreneurship.

Earnings figures are estimates only and are not guaranteed. Actual income will vary based on individual effort, hours worked, location and market conditions. See our NYC barber salary guide for the data behind these ranges.

FAQs

Thinking About the Leap? Your Questions, Answered

New York requires 500 hours of state-approved training, then passing the NY State Board Exam to earn your Master Barber license. At ABI you complete those hours hands-on in about 4 months full-time or 6–7 months on weekends, with full board-exam prep and job-placement support. Our complete NY roadmap walks through every step.

New York State requires 500 hours of training. Full-time at ABI takes about 4 months (17 weeks at 30 hours per week); the weekend schedule takes about 6–7 months (27 weeks).

ABI's 500-hour Master Barber program is $5,600 for the morning plan (Plan A) or $4,600 for the afternoon (Plan B) and weekend (Plan C) plans. Every plan uses weekly payments while you train. Books and tools are extra. ACCES-VR funding and Post-9/11 GI Bill® / VA benefits are accepted.

No experience is needed. ABI trains you from the fundamentals to a licensed professional — many students arrive as complete beginners or career-changers with no background in barbering.

Yes. ABI offers morning (8:00 AM–2:00 PM) and afternoon (2:00 PM–8:00 PM) full-time tracks Monday–Friday, plus a weekend track on Saturdays and Sundays for people who work during the week. See schedules & flexibility for the full breakdown.

Yes — ACCES-VR can cover tuition, tools and books for qualified New Yorkers with disabilities; Post-9/11 GI Bill® and VA benefits are accepted; and every plan includes weekly payments while you train. Learn more on tuition & funding.
