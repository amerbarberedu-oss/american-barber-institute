Paying for Barber Training in New York

Barber school in New York typically runs a few thousand dollars for the required 500-hour program, and most students never pay it all at once. Between weekly payment plans, GI Bill® benefits for veterans, and ACCES-VR vocational funding for eligible New Yorkers, the real out-of-pocket cost is often far lower than the sticker price. This hub breaks down every way to fund your training and where to get exact numbers.

The Short Answer

What does barber school cost — and how do people pay for it?

A New York barber program covering the state-required 500 hours generally falls in the low-to-mid four figures depending on the school and schedule. Very few students write one check. The common funding paths are: (1) a weekly or monthly payment plan spread across the program, (2) GI Bill® benefits if you're a veteran or eligible dependent, and (3) ACCES-VR if you qualify for vocational rehabilitation support. Grants, scholarships, and family help fill the rest.

How Tuition Is Structured

Understanding the "Plan" Concept

Most established barber schools price a single 500-hour program but offer more than one payment structure — often labeled as plans. The total number of training hours is the same for everyone (New York requires 500 to sit for the license); the plans simply change how and when you pay, and sometimes which schedule you're on.

At American Barber Institute, for example, tuition is offered as three plans — Plan A at $5,600, and Plans B and C at $4,600 each — so you can match the payment approach to your budget and schedule. Because exact figures, deposits, and weekly terms can change, always confirm current pricing directly with the school before you enroll.

Common Questions

Tuition & Funding FAQ

Can I pay for barber school weekly instead of all at once?

Yes. Most New York barber schools, including ABI, offer payment plans that spread tuition across the length of the program rather than requiring the full amount upfront. Confirm the specific weekly or monthly terms with the school when you enroll.

Does financial aid (FAFSA) cover barber school?

Federal Title IV aid depends on the school's accreditation status. Many barber programs rely instead on payment plans, GI Bill® benefits, and ACCES-VR funding. Ask each school directly which funding sources it accepts before assuming FAFSA applies.

Are barber school tools included in tuition?

It varies by school. Some bundle a starter kit into tuition; others sell tools separately or expect you to bring your own. Our beginner's tool kit guide explains what you'll need either way.

How much does the New York license itself cost?

State application and examination fees are paid to New York — separate from school tuition. Current amounts are published by the New York Department of State, Division of Licensing Services.
