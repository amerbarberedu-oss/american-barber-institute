Full-Time or Weekend: How to Choose Your 500-Hour Schedule

The 500 hours to a New York barber license are the same no matter how you space them. The real decision is pace — finish fast full-time, or train around a job on weekends. Here is how to pick the schedule that actually fits your life.

Full-Time~4 Months
Weekend~6–7 Months
Hours Either Way500
Tuition Range$4,600–$5,600

The Short Answer

Which schedule should you pick?

Choose full-time if training can be your main focus right now — you'll finish the 500 hours in about 4 months. Choose the weekend schedule if you need to keep a weekday job — you'll finish in roughly 6–7 months, training Saturday and Sunday. Both cover the identical 500-hour curriculum and prepare you for the same New York State Board exam. The only real trade-off is time versus income during training.

Not sure the 500-hour route is even right for you yet? Start with the overview of the full 500-hour path, then come back here to lock in a schedule.

Common Questions

Choosing a Schedule, Answered

Does the weekend schedule cost more because it takes longer?
No. The weekend plan (Plan C) is $4,600 — the same as the full-time afternoon plan. You are paying for the same 500 hours; spreading them over more calendar weeks does not raise the tuition.
Can I switch schedules after I start?
Because every schedule covers the identical 500-hour curriculum, moving between full-time and weekend cohorts is usually possible if your life changes mid-program. Confirm the specifics with admissions when you enroll.
Which schedule gets me licensed sooner?
Full-time. You'll complete the 500 hours in about 4 months versus roughly 6–7 months on weekends — but weekend students give up no weekday income along the way.
When do classes start?
New cohorts begin the first Monday of each month, so you rarely wait long to start on either schedule. Next start: soon.
