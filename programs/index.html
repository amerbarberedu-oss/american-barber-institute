Which Path to a New York Barber License Fits You?

There isn't one way to become a licensed barber in New York — there are three, and the right one depends entirely on where you're starting from. Use this page to find yours in under a minute.

The Short Answer

Start where you are

Complete beginner? You need the 500-hour route. Already licensed in cosmetology, or an apprentice? A 50-hour refresher may be enough. Applying for or renewing a barber license? You'll also need the 3-hour Contagious Diseases course. Pick the card that matches your situation below.

Compare at a Glance

The Three Paths, Side by Side

By Experience

500-hour = no prior license. 50-hour = already licensed. 3-hour = a requirement everyone applying or renewing must meet.

By Time

Roughly 4 months full-time or 6–7 on weekends for the 500 hours; about two weeks for the refresher; three self-paced hours by mail for the diseases course.

By Cost

500-hour tuition runs $4,600–$5,600, the refresher is $1,500, and the Contagious Diseases course is $100.

Same Destination

Every path leads to the same place: sitting for the New York State Board exam and earning a Master Barber license.

Monthly Starts

New 500-hour and refresher cohorts begin the first Monday of each month, so you're rarely waiting long to begin.

Funding Exists

Veterans may use the GI Bill® and eligible career-changers may qualify for ACCES-VR toward the 500-hour route.
